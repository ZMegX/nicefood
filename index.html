Nice, on France's southeast coast along the Mediterranean, has a rich and varied past shaped over ages by several civilisations.
Originally established by the Greeks circa 350 BC and bearing Nikaia after the Greek goddess of victory, the city subsequently grew to be a major Roman colony.
